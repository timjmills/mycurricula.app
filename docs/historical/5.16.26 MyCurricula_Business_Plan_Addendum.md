## XVIII. Brand & Name Lockdown — Immediate Next Steps

**Name decided: MyCurricula. Primary domain secured: mycurricula.app.**

The name is substantially more registrable than the prior working title, but still sits in the descriptive zone — "curricula" is the plural of the literal category noun. Comparable marks (MyFitnessPal, MyChart) reached the USPTO Principal Register but typically only after years of substantially exclusive use established acquired distinctiveness under Lanham Act §2(f). Expect a similar path: initial filing may be offered the Supplemental Register first, then upgradeable to the Principal Register after ~5 years of substantially exclusive use. The Supplemental Register is still useful — it gives you ®, blocks confusingly similar marks, and creates a placeholder for later Principal Register promotion.

### Trademark filing — timing matters more than urgency

**File the USPTO trademark application ~30–60 days before public paid launch** (estimated mid-2027), not now. Reasons:

- The mark must be either in use in commerce (real product + real customers + specimen of use) or filed as Intent-to-Use (ITU).
- ITU filings cost an extra $100/class plus require a Statement of Use within 6 months (extensions available for additional fees).
- Filing too early without a committed launch burns the 6-month ITU window and racks up extension fees.
- Filing too late means somebody else could file first in your category and force you to negotiate or rename.

**The 60-day-pre-launch window is the sweet spot.** By then product, screenshots, and marketing assets exist as specimens of use, and you'll qualify for a use-based filing.

### Trademark filing logistics (target: mid-2027)

- **USPTO TEAS Plus filing**: ~$250 per class. File in **both** Class 9 (downloadable software) and Class 42 (SaaS for educators) = **~$500 in filing fees**.
- **Attorney-assisted filing** (strongly recommended for descriptive-zone marks): $1,000–$2,500 additional. A flat-fee trademark attorney (Cognition IP, LegalForce, or a solo IP practitioner) will draft the description of goods/services and respond to Office Actions — both highly likely for marks in the descriptive zone.
- **Madrid Protocol international filing**: defer until $50K+ ARR. Filing starts ~$650 + per-country fees.
- **Time to ®**: 12–18 months from filing to registration in a clean case; 18–30 months if any Office Action is issued.

### Backup-name optionality

Have **2 backup-domain registrations** as cheap insurance in case USPTO blocks MyCurricula on the Principal Register and you decide a coined/arbitrary mark is worth the rebrand cost later. Verify availability before committing — common candidates include `pacely.app`, `forka.app`, `coursely.app`, `praxio.app`, `curriculate.app`. Spending $30–$60 now on two defensive registrations is cheap optionality.

### Brand-asset minimums for launch (target: mid-2027)

- **Wordmark** ("MyCurricula" in a chosen typeface): $0 DIY or $200–$600 commissioned
- **Logo / app icon / favicon**: $200–$800 on 99designs or directly with a freelancer
- **Color palette + typography spec**: codify in a single Figma or Notion page
- **One-sentence positioning line** to test on teachers during the Phase 1B closed beta — e.g., *"MyCurricula — the collaborative lesson planner with version control."*
- **90-second product demo video** for the homepage above the fold (Loom or Riverside, recorded in the actual product)

### What happens if USPTO refuses Principal Register

Most likely outcome for "MyCurricula" given descriptive-zone classification:

1. **Office Action issued**: Examining Attorney may issue a §2(e)(1) descriptiveness refusal.
2. **Response options**:
   - Argue acquired distinctiveness under §2(f) — unlikely to succeed early
   - Amend to **Supplemental Register** — easiest path, gives you ®, costs no extra
   - Argue the mark is suggestive rather than descriptive — possible with attorney help
   - Abandon and refile a new mark
3. **Supplemental Register is fine for years 1–5.** You can refile for Principal Register after demonstrating ~5 years of substantially exclusive and continuous use under §2(f).
4. **Practical effect**: a Supplemental Register registration still appears in TESS, deters knock-off filings, and supports cease-and-desist letters against confusing competitors.
