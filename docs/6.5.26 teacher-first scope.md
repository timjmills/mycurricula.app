## 0. Goal & product framing

Invert the product from **school-first** to **teacher-first**.

- Sold to **individual teachers**. The **teacher is the root entity** and owns their
  curriculum from the moment they sign up ‚Äî **no school/org wrapper required, ever**.
- **Open signup** with **both Google and email/password** sign-in.
- Every teacher gets their **own private curriculum by default**.
- **Teams form by invite** (link or email) ‚Äî an opt-in shared space.
- A **"school"** is an **optional future grouping** that may buy seats team-wide later.
  We keep the `schools` table for this, dormant, but never force it on a teacher.
- **The Master/Personal forking model must not break.**

The current codebase is school-first (`teacher ‚Üí school ‚Üí grade`, curriculum hangs off
`grade_levels.school_id NOT NULL`, fail-closed email-domain provisioning). This scope
inverts that. Where this doc says "school-first" it only names the legacy model being
removed.

## 1. Resolved decisions (owner sign-off 2026-06-05)

1. **Solo = Master in a personal space.** A solo teacher's curriculum is stored as
   **Master rows** in their own personal space, with the teacher auto-granted
   master-edit rights ‚Äî NOT as `personal_authored_lessons` and NOT as self-forks.
   *(This is the linchpin ‚Äî see ¬ß2.)*
2. **Email must be verified before a curriculum space is created.** Google arrives
   pre-verified; email/password signups provision only on first **verified** entry.
3. **Invite links are single-use but regeneratable**, bounded by the seat cap (¬ß3).
4. **Any member (up to the 5-account cap) can invite.**
5. **Keep the `schools` table** as the dormant future-org layer (documented in ¬ß7).

### Seat / plan model (new business rule, baked into this scope)

- **One master curriculum = one team = up to 5 associated accounts.**
- **1 paid account** (the owner/creator of the master curriculum) **+ up to 4 free
  accounts** joined by invite = **5 total seats** per team.
- The cap is enforced on **invite creation** and again **atomically on redeem**
  (so concurrent redeems can't overshoot).
- **Any** of the (up to 5) members may generate/regenerate a single-use invite link
  while the team is **under** the cap.
- Removing a member **frees a seat** (the count drops; a new invite can be issued).
- **Pending (un-redeemed) invites count against the cap** so a member can't issue 10
  links for 1 open seat and have them all redeem.
- **Any member can rescind (revoke) a pending invite**, which **immediately frees the
  held seat** (status ‚Üí `revoked`; a revoked token can never redeem). This is the relief
  valve for the pending-counts-against-cap rule: if all seats are held by outstanding
  invites, rescind one to open a slot.
- **Billing itself is NOT built in this phase** ‚Äî only the **seat count cap of 5** is
  enforced. The "1 paid / 4 free" distinction is recorded (owner = the paid seat) but
  payment/plan management is a later slice. A future **school** plan raises/replaces the
  per-team cap via `teams.school_id` + `teams.seat_limit`.

## 2. The linchpin: store solo curriculum as Master

The forking **tables do not change**. What is school-first is the **authorization
layer** wrapped around them. The single highest-leverage decision is how a solo
teacher's curriculum is stored:

> **Solo curriculum = Master rows in the teacher's own personal space, with the
> teacher holding master-edit rights for their own subjects.**

Why this is make-or-break:

- **Solo‚Üíteam becomes additive.** When a solo teacher invites peers, the invitees
  simply get membership rows, read that existing Master, and fork their own Personal
  copies ‚Äî exactly as the model already works. **No content migration.**
- The alternatives are costly:
  - `personal_authored_lessons` ‚Üí invitees see an empty Master; promotion requires a
    real ETL (rebuild `units` FKs, flip `lesson_sections.owner_kind`
    `personal_authored ‚Üí master`, move completion from a free-text `status` column into
    `completion_status`). Data-loss-prone.
  - Self-lazy-forks ‚Üí the solo teacher's own cards falsely render with the **dashed
    "Modified" stripe** (CLAUDE.md ¬ß2) against a Master they themselves wrote.

**Planner/UX consequence:** the **Personal / Team Curriculum** toggle is meaningless
with one member (master == personal). In a solo space, **hide or pin it to a single
"My Curriculum" mode** that writes Master directly (`saveTarget: "core"`), and use a
**solo variant** of the always-on tooltip copy (the stock "changes affect the whole
team" is wrong when there is no team). Flip to team language only once a second member
joins.

## 3. Data model changes

Keep all forking tables untouched. **Reuse `teacher_grade_assignments` (TGA)** as the
membership + RLS junction ‚Äî do **not** replace it (every read helper keys off it).
Add a thin teacher-first ownership layer on top.

### New tables

- **`teams`** ‚Äî the optional shared space / master-curriculum unit.
  - `id`, `name`, `created_by_teacher_id` (the founding owner = paid seat),
  - `school_id uuid NULL references schools(id) on delete set null` ‚Äî **dormant future
    "school buys seats" hook**,
  - `seat_limit int not null default 5` ‚Äî the seat cap (¬ß1),
  - per-tenant config moved off `schools`: `school_week weekday[]`,
    `ramadan_timetable_enabled`, `resource_hosting_mode`,
  - `created_at` / `updated_at` (+ `set_updated_at` trigger).
- **`team_memberships`** ‚Äî invite-granted membership (thin grouping over TGA).
  - `team_id`, `teacher_id`, `role grade_role` (reuse existing enum), `unique(team_id, teacher_id)`.
  - TGA remains the per-grade RLS-authoritative record; this expresses "member of team
    B but only invited to grade X."
- **`invitations`** ‚Äî invite lifecycle.
  - `token_hash` (store a hash, never the raw token ‚Äî raw shown to inviter once),
  - `target_grade_level_id` (+ `target_team_id`), `role grade_role`,
  - `inviter_teacher_id`, `invitee_email` (NULL = link invite; set = email invite,
    redeemer email must match),
  - `status` enum (`pending|accepted|revoked|expired`), `expires_at`,
    `accepted_at`, `accepted_by`,
  - indexes on `token_hash` (unique), target grade, inviter, `lower(invitee_email)`,
    partial pending.
  - Add `audit_action` enum values: `invite_created/accepted/revoked/expired/resent`.

### Changed tables

- **`grade_levels`**: add `owner_teacher_id` (solo) + `team_id` (shared);
  `school_id` ‚Üí **NULLABLE**, FK `on delete cascade` ‚Üí **`set null`**;
  XOR check `((owner_teacher_id is not null) <> (team_id is not null))`
  (added `NOT VALID`, validated after backfill); indexes on both new cols.
- **`school_years`**: same owner/team/nullable treatment (units require a school-year;
  a solo teacher needs a personal one).
- **`teachers`**: `school_id` ‚Üí **NULLABLE** (a teacher needs no org). The column-level
  update grant from `20260604140000` stays ‚Äî `school_id` is never client-updatable.
- **Forking core unchanged** (`master_core_lesson_events`,
  `personal_core_lesson_event_copies`, `personal_authored_lessons`, `lesson_sections`,
  `completion_status`) ‚Äî all reach tenancy via `grade_level_id` and inherit the new
  ownership once `grade_levels` is teacher/team-ownable.

### Signup provisioning (no school required)

A `SECURITY DEFINER` function (replacing the school-resolution body of
`ensure-teacher.ts`) creates, idempotently, on first **verified** sign-in:
a `teachers` row (`school_id NULL`), one owned `grade_levels` row, a personal
`school_years` row, the **8 locked subjects** (same set/colors as seed), a
`teacher_grade_assignments(role 'lead')` self-assignment, and **self
`subject_team_memberships(can_edit_master=true)`** for the teacher's own subjects (so
they can author their own Master per ¬ß2). No domain check, no school, no lockout.

## 4. Auth ‚Äî Google + email/password

- Both providers funnel through the **same server-side provisioning** (mirror the
  `/auth/gsi` posture shipped in PR #5) so neither bypasses isolation.
- New surfaces: signup form (tabs on `/login` + a small `/signup`), **email
  verification** handling (redeems through the existing `/auth/callback`), forgot/reset
  password (`/forgot-password`, `/reset-password`), and a password **server route**
  (no client-side `signInWithPassword` ‚Äî keep provisioning + cookies server-gated).
- **Email verification gates space creation** (decision #2): `signUp` creates only the
  `auth.users` row; the curriculum space is provisioned on first verified entry.
- Open-signup hardening (no more domain gate): Supabase email-confirm ON, **CAPTCHA**
  on signup + reset, Auth **rate limits**, generic "if an account exists‚Ä¶" responses
  (no email enumeration), reuse `safeRelativePath` for all redirect targets, copy the
  `origin`-check/CSRF guard from the gsi route.

## 5. Retiring the domain allow-list ‚Äî no lockout

- Provisioning inverts from "resolve email domain ‚Üí school or **refuse**" to
  "**find-or-create this teacher's own private space**." Everyone gets in, isolated to
  their own data.
- **Existing beta teachers keep working untouched** ‚Äî their `teachers` + TGA rows
  already exist; provisioning is idempotent and never re-homes them.
- **Backfill** turns the current Grade-5 school into a **team owned by the lead**, every
  current member auto-added to `team_memberships`, TGA rows left intact ‚Üí RLS verdicts
  identical the instant after. Deterministic lead pick (`role='lead'`/`grade_admin`,
  else earliest assignment). Migration asserts no half-migrated rows.
- `ALLOWED_TEACHER_EMAIL_DOMAINS` / `CLAUDE_PROVISION_EMAIL_DOMAINS` are **retired**;
  the schema change + provisioning change must **ship together**. The allow-list concept
  survives only as an **optional per-team auto-join** convenience (a future school
  setting), not the access gate.

## 6. Invites, teams, and RLS

**What survives unchanged:** because reads already gate per-grade via TGA
(`can_read_grade` / `auth_teacher_grade_ids`), most RLS is untouched ‚Äî we change *how
rows enter TGA* (invite redemption), not what reads them. "Invited to grade X but not a
team's other grades" already holds.

**Redeem flow:** `/invite/[token]` ‚Üí sign in/up (Google or email/pw) ‚Üí a
`SECURITY DEFINER` RPC `redeem_invitation(token)` validates token hash + `status=pending`
+ not expired + email match (email invites) + **seat cap not exceeded**, then inserts the
TGA + `team_memberships` rows (and `subject_team_memberships` **only** for
collaborator/lead roles). It is the only non-admin path that writes TGA.

**Three must-fix RLS items (the real risk):**

1. **`teachers_read` / `resources_read` use "same school"** (`auth_teacher_school_id`) ‚Äî
   leaks across teacher-first spaces. **Re-scope to co-membership** (new helper
   `shares_grade_with(teacher_id)`).
2. **Redeem must NOT default `can_edit_subject_master = true`.** The column **defaults
   true** ‚Äî a careless redeem silently grants every invitee Master-write. Set it
   explicitly per role: **viewer** = read + fork only (no STM rows); **collaborator/lead**
   = `can_edit_master=true`.
3. **`teacher_grade_assignments` write stays admin/RPC-only** ‚Äî no authenticated
   self-insert policy; redemption is the only non-admin write, via the validated RPC.
   The existing `tga_write` same-school `WITH CHECK` must be relaxed/adjusted for the
   invite path (or the invite RPC, as definer, bypasses it).

**Seat-cap enforcement:** check at invite-create (`active members + pending invites <
seat_limit`) and re-check atomically inside `redeem_invitation` (count under
`teams.seat_limit`, default 5). **Pending invites count against the cap; rescinding
(revoking) a pending invite frees the held seat immediately.** Any member may create
invites while under cap (#4), and any member may revoke a pending invite (the Team page
"revoke" action sets `status='revoked'`).

**Invite-role mapping:** viewer ‚Üí `grade_role 'teacher'`, no STM (read + fork only);
collaborator ‚Üí `'teacher'` + STM `can_edit_master=true`; lead ‚Üí `'lead'` + STM.

**UI surfaces** (Settings hub, single-purpose per CLAUDE.md ¬ß4):
- `app/settings/team` ‚Äî member list (name, role, seat usage e.g. "3 / 5"), pending
  invites (revoke/resend), invite panel (link or email + role).
- Standalone `app/invite/[token]` accept screen (an invitee may belong to nothing yet);
  handles unauth/expired/revoked/already-accepted/email-mismatch/**seat-full** states.
- Consequential controls (invite, role, remove, Join) use **always-on** tooltips
  (`required: true`); others use the dismissible W2-B3 system. No confirm dialogs;
  removing a member explains (via required tooltip) that their Personal forks become
  unreadable to them but are not deleted.

## 7. The `schools` table (kept, documented)

`schools`, `school_admins`, and `is_school_admin()` are **retained but dormant** for
solo/team-only usage. They are the **future "a school buys team-wide seats" layer**:
- `teams.school_id` (nullable) is the claim hook ‚Äî a school sets it to adopt teams.
- `grade_levels.school_id` / `school_years.school_id` stay nullable-but-populated for
  the backfilled beta team, preserving the school link without forcing it on anyone.
- Do **not** delete these tables ‚Äî they are load-bearing for the beta backfill and for
  catalog/framework/reminder scoping. Schools become a real product surface in a later
  phase (admin console, seats, billing).

|---|---|---|---|
| Solo‚Üíteam promotion rebuild | Low* | High | *Eliminated by decision #1 (solo = Master). Add a test asserting a solo space has zero personal-copy rows. |
