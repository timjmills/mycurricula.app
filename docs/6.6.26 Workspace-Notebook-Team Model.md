## 1. The three nouns

- **Workspace** — the "roof." A school/org **or** just you. Holds one or more Notebooks
  and carries the things that are shared *across* notebooks: the academic calendar,
  holidays, the school week, the member/seat list, and (later) billing.
- **Notebook** — one **curriculum** (Grade 5, Music, Art Tutoring). Lives inside a
  Workspace. Has its own subjects, lessons, and schedule. Shared with a group **or**
  solo.
- **Team / Personal** — the two content layers *inside* a Notebook:
  - **Team** = the shared plan everyone on the notebook sees (the "Master"/"Core").
  - **Personal** = your forks of shared lessons + your private subjects/lessons.

> **Why "Team/Personal" and not "Master/Personal":** the database already speaks this —
> `subjects.scope` is literally `'team' | 'personal'`, and the tooltip spec
> (`CLAUDE.md` §4) already calls the top-bar control the "Personal / Team Curriculum"
> toggle. "Team" is warmer than "Master" and matches the data. **UI copy migrates
> `Master` → `Team`;** the git-style words (`Main`/`Fork`) stay in code, never in the UI.

## 2. The hierarchy

```
Account (you — one login, one bill)
 └─ Workspace            ← a school/org, or just you  (= a `schools` row)
     └─ Notebook         ← one curriculum             (= a `grade_levels` row + its subjects)
         ├─ Team          (the shared plan)           (master rows; subjects.scope='team')
         └─ Personal      (your forks + private adds)  (personal copies; scope='personal')
```

You can have **many Notebooks in one Workspace**, and (future) **many Workspaces** under
one Account.

## 3. Two different axes (don't conflate them)

- **Axis A — inside one Notebook:** Team vs Personal (the forking model). Shipped in
  Phase 1A as visual + state; persistence is the Phase 1B Supabase wave.
- **Axis B — across Notebooks/Workspaces:** separate curricula, some shared, some solo,
  under one paid account. This is the *outer* layer; the multi-notebook / multi-workspace
  parts are **deferred** (see §7).

## 4. Why two container layers (Workspace *and* Notebook)

Two layers exist because two different groups don't line up: **who shares the calendar**
(the whole school) vs **who co-writes a given curriculum** (that grade's people). One
layer can't express both without forcing duplication.

- **Workspace = configure once, applies to all.** A holiday/snow-day added at the
  Workspace shifts every Notebook's calendar; a teacher added once can be slotted into
  any Notebook.
- **Notebook = scoped content + participation.** Each curriculum has its own plan; who
  participates in which notebook is per-notebook.
- **Collapse to one layer and you pay in duplication** either way — N calendars to keep
  in sync, or per-notebook re-invites. The second layer is what lets you maintain
  calendar/holidays/members **once**.
- **A private Notebook is just a shared one with a team of one.** Solo → shared is
  additive (invite someone; your content *becomes* the Team layer; they fork). No
  migration — this is the linchpin (`scope` doc §2, ultraplan §0).
- **A layer should be invisible until it's doing work.** For a solo teacher the Workspace
  recedes (auto-created, "your space," unmanaged); it earns visible attention only with
  multiple notebooks/people.

## 5. Function of each

| Concept | Who sees / edits | What config lives here |
|---|---|---|
| **Workspace** | the member group (≤5 seats today) | calendar, holidays, school week, member/seat list, billing (later) |
| **Notebook (shared)** | members assigned to it | subjects, lessons, schedule for that curriculum |
| **Notebook (private)** | just you | same, but team-of-one |
| **Team layer** | everyone on the notebook | the single source of truth; editing is the gated, banner-guarded action |
| **Personal layer** | only you | your forks + private subjects; never touches Team unless you push |

## 6. Data-model mapping (vocabulary → committed tables)

| Vocabulary | Table(s) | Notes / source |
|---|---|---|
| **Workspace** | `schools` | The hidden per-team container (ultraplan §2 "Strategy A"). Not shown in the UI today. |
| **Workspace member group / seats** | `teams` (+ `team_memberships`) | `teams.school_id` is **`NOT NULL UNIQUE`** → **1:1 with the workspace** (`20260606120000_teams_invitations.sql:74`). Seat cap `seat_cap=5`. |
| **Notebook** | `grade_levels` (+ its `subjects`) | One per workspace today (provisioning seeds `'My Class'`). The original schema already allows **many `grade_levels` per `schools`** — the multi-notebook hook (no new column needed; `grade_levels` was intentionally **not** altered, `20260606120000:18`). |
| **Workspace membership** | `team_memberships` | The seat ledger (`team_id`,`teacher_id`,`role`). |
| **Notebook participation** | `teacher_grade_assignments` (TGA) | Per-grade; what `can_read_grade()` keys off. Splitting members across notebooks within one workspace is just per-grade TGA. |
| **Team content layer** | `master_core_lesson_events`, `subjects.scope='team'` | Master-edit gated per-subject by `subject_team_memberships.can_edit_master`. |
| **Personal content layer** | `personal_core_lesson_event_copies`, `personal_authored_lessons`, `subjects.scope='personal'` | Lazy per-teacher forks + private authored lessons. |

**Naming-collision check (resolved):** the `teams` *table* is the **Workspace-level**
member/seat group (1:1 school today); the **Team** *content layer* is the shared plan
inside a Notebook. These are the same idea at two granularities ("the people you share
with" vs "content shared with them") — coherent, not conflicting.

## 7. Current state — "are we set?"

**Set on foundation and direction; the convergence step is the immediate next wave.**

**Built & reviewed (Waves 1–4):**
- Control plane: `teams` / `team_memberships` / `invitations` + RPCs (`create_invite`,
  `redeem_invite`, `revoke_invite`, `expire_invitations`) with hashed tokens, atomic
  seat-cap (5), per-role master-edit, RLS.
- Solo provisioning: `provision_individual_workspace` seeds a full solo workspace
  (school + grade + 8 locked subjects + school_year + `teachers.school_id` + owner team
  + membership).
- **Notebook-level sharing works:** `redeem_invite` grants a TGA → the invitee reads the
  team's Team/Master plan and forks Personal copies (`20260606140000_invite_lifecycle.sql:494`).

**The v1 collapse:** today every teacher is **1 Workspace = 1 Team = 1 Notebook**
(provisioning makes exactly one of each). That is precisely the "private notebook /
single shared notebook" case — correct for v1, not a contradiction of this model.

**NOT yet wired (the next wave — the workspace-level convergence):**
- `redeem_invite` **deliberately does not rewrite `teachers.school_id`** to the team's
  workspace (its own boundary comment, `20260606140000:54–67`; see `:519–520`). So today
  an invitee sees the shared **notebook's lessons** (grade-scoped, via TGA) but **not**
  workspace-scoped data — teammate list, the shared `school_years`, frameworks/resources
  — because those gate on `auth_teacher_school_id()`, still their own solo school.
- The **auth-path hook** ("invite-accept takes precedence over auto-workspace-creation")
  is unbuilt, so a fresh invitee currently also gets a throwaway solo workspace.
  `redeem_invite` is written to be safely re-runnable so the hook can call it after/
  instead of workspace creation.
- User-facing surfaces (magic-link send/confirm, `/invite/[token]`, `settings/team`) are
  the upcoming UI wave.

**Net:** the shared-*notebook* promise (read Master + fork) is live; the shared-
*workspace* experience (teammate list + shared calendar/frameworks) and a clean
no-throwaway invite land in the convergence wave. Nothing here blocks it.

## 8. Future expansions — what each one costs

| Expansion | Blocked? | What it needs |
|---|---|---|
| **Make the Workspace visible** | No | UI only — surface the hidden `schools` container as "Workspace"; today it's invisible by design. |
| **Multi-notebook per workspace** | No | Provisioning + UI to create additional `grade_levels` (notebooks) under one `schools` row and a notebook switcher. Invitations already target a specific grade (`target_grade_level_id`); per-notebook membership is TGA. Schema already allows many grades per school. |
| **Multi-workspace per teacher** (Maria/James/Aisha in §examples) | No, but not free | The scalar `teachers.school_id` (used by `auth_teacher_school_id()` for school-scoped reads) must become **junction-derived co-membership** — the scope doc's "must-fix #1" that Strategy A deferred (R4). The many-to-many bones exist (TGA, `team_memberships`); the work is re-scoping school-scoped RLS off the scalar column. Schema + policy change. |
| **Billing / paid vs free seats** | No | Only the seat **count** (5) is enforced now; payment gating + a school plan that raises `teams.seat_cap` via `teams.school_id` is a later slice. |

## 9. Rules / do-nots

- **Keep "Team/Personal" matching `subjects.scope`** (`'team' | 'personal'`). Don't
  reintroduce "Master/Core" in UI copy; migrate it to **Team**. Keep `Main`/`Fork`
  (git metaphor) in code only — never in the UI.
- **Don't say "school" in the teacher-facing UI.** The `schools` row is the hidden
  Workspace container; the school *product* (admin console, buying seats) is later-phase
  and stays dormant (`scope` doc §7).
- **Don't break the forking model** (`CLAUDE.md` §2): solo content is **Team/Master
  rows in the teacher's own space**, never self-forks — so solo→shared stays additive.
- **Let the Workspace recede for solo users** — auto-created, unmanaged, "your space";
  flip to team language only once a 2nd member joins (`scope` doc §2 solo-UX rule).
- **Don't assume one notebook or one workspace** in new data shapes/queries, even while
  v1 ships the 1:1:1 collapse — mirror the grade-scoping rule in `CLAUDE.md` §5.
