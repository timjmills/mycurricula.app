Specifications

01 · Forking model

Fork diff view — "What's different from Master?"

P1Phase 1B

The three-tier stripe system tells a teacher that a lesson diverged, but not what changed. A diff on the lesson detail turns the git metaphor into a usable tool — the single biggest payoff on the product's core differentiator.

Design spec

  • Entry point: a "Compare with Team Curriculum" action on any modified lesson — in the lesson detail header and the card's overflow menu. Never shown on unedited lessons.
  • Inline diff, not side-by-side, as the default: changed fields render old → new within the lesson detail layout the teacher already knows. Side-by-side is a secondary toggle for long content fields.
  • Field-level granularity: title, objective, materials, flow steps, standards, and scheduling (day/order) each diff independently. A moved-only lesson shows just the scheduling row.
  • Diff coloring uses the semantic layer, not subject colors: removed in --danger-tint, added in --done-tint — consistent on every subject.
  • Two actions, both undoable (see 02): Revert to Team version (drops the fork) and Propose to Team (opens Master mode pre-filled with the personal version; the existing red banner is the safety mechanism — no confirm dialog).
  • Per-field revert on hover for surgical un-forking, mirroring the hover-revealed-controls pattern from the Weekly card.

Surfaces affected

Daily · lesson detail Weekly · card menu Catch-up · triage rows

Acceptance criteria

  • A teacher can see every divergent field of a modified lesson in under two clicks from the Weekly grid.
  • Revert (whole-lesson and per-field) restores the Master value and updates the stripe/pill state immediately, with undo.
  • Propose to Team never writes to Master without the explicit toggle + banner flow.
  • Diff renders correctly for all three divergence tiers: modified, moved, both.

Depends on: Supabase persistence of fork lineage (Phase 1B). Design now: the diff layout and the revert/propose interaction are pure design problems — prototype before the backend lands.

02 · Safety without friction

Undo toasts instead of irreversibility

P1Phase 1A.5

The product correctly avoids confirm dialogs — the complement to "no friction going in" is "easy way back out." A consistent undo toast makes lazy forking feel safe rather than mysterious.

Design spec

  • One toast component, bottom-center, white surface with hairline border and --sh-md; a single text line + an "Undo" text button. Auto-dismisses after 6 seconds; timer pauses on hover/focus.
  • Fires on: lesson move ("Moved to Tuesday — Undo"), completion toggle, first-time fork ("Saved to your personal plan — Undo"), revert, and bulk shift (see 06).
  • First-time fork copy doubles as just-in-time education — it is the moment the forking model explains itself. Voice: sticky-note brief.
  • Only one toast visible at a time; a new action replaces the previous toast (last-in wins). No stacking, no notification center.
  • Keyboard: ⌘Z / Ctrl+Z triggers the live toast's undo while it is visible.
  • Respects prefers-reduced-motion: fades without sliding.

Surfaces affected

Global · app shell Weekly Daily Catch-up

Acceptance criteria

  • Every destructive or state-changing gesture in Weekly/Daily/Catch-up offers undo for ≥6 seconds.
  • Undo of a first-time fork removes the personal copy entirely — stripe returns to solid, pill disappears.
  • Toast never obscures the drag target area or sticky chrome at any viewport tier; ≥44px hit target on the Undo action.

Depends on: nothing — works against client state today, then becomes the optimistic-update surface when Supabase lands. Build the pattern once, before 1B.

03 · Orientation

A "now" anchor on Weekly and Daily

P1Phase 1A.5

The core job is "what are we teaching this week, and where am I in the plan?" The UI should answer where am I without scrolling.

Design spec

  • Weekly grid: today's column gets a quiet emphasis — a "Today" label chip in the column header and a faint warm wash (--surface-warm) on the column. No saturated color; subject color stays the loudest voice.
  • Daily view: on load, the day pane positions to the current period (container scroll, never scrollIntoView). A 1px indigo "now" line with a small time chip sits at the current time.
  • The now line derives its position from the teacher's configured schedule — including rotating A/B cycles — never from a hard-coded timetable.
  • Off-hours and non-school days: no line; the Today chip alone carries orientation. Holidays (once rendered) suppress both.
  • A persistent "Today" button in the view header jumps back when the teacher has navigated away — same affordance on Weekly (current week) and Year (current month).

Surfaces affected

Weekly · grid header + column Daily · day pane Year · roadmap

Acceptance criteria

  • Opening /daily during school hours lands the current period in view with zero scrolling.
  • Today emphasis derives from the configured school week — correct on Sun–Thu, Mon–Fri, and custom weeks.
  • The now line is invisible in print output.

Depends on: nothing — schedule config already exists in Phase 1A. Smallest item on this list; ship first.

04 · Navigation

Command palette / quick jump (⌘K)

P2Phase 1B

"Filter everywhere" is a core principle; a palette that jumps to Week 14 · Math, a unit, a standard, or a settings page makes the whole app feel fast. Teachers planning on Sunday night are power users.

Design spec

  • Opens with ⌘K / Ctrl+K or the search affordance in the top bar. Centered overlay, --r-xl panel, --sh-lg, pill-shaped input.
  • Result groups in order: Jump to (weeks, units, lessons), Views, Standards, Settings. Lesson results carry their subject color as a leading dot — color stays navigation.
  • Query grammar matches the domain vocabulary: "week 14", "math unit 3", "CCSS 5.NBT.5", "ramadan timetable". Middle-dot result format: Week 14 · Math · Multiply by 2-digit numbers.
  • Recent destinations shown on empty query — the palette doubles as "recently viewed."
  • Fully keyboard-driven: arrows + Enter; Esc closes. Focus returns to the invoking element.
  • Phone/tablet: same surface presented as a full-height sheet from the search icon; no keyboard shortcut dependency.

Surfaces affected

Global · top bar All views · destinations

Acceptance criteria

  • Any week, unit, lesson, or settings page reachable in ≤3 keystrokes + Enter from anywhere.
  • Results respect grade scoping and the Personal/Master viewing mode.
  • Works at all three viewport tiers; touch path requires no hardware keyboard.

Depends on: deep links (07) for destinations; search index is trivial against mock data, real with Supabase. Pairs naturally with the 1B wave.

05 · Scheduling integrity

Holiday- and conflict-aware drag & drop

P2Phase 1B

Dropping a lesson on a holiday or non-school day should offer "next instructional day" rather than failing silently. Pairs with the Phase 1B holiday work.

Design spec

  • Non-instructional columns (holidays, weekends in view, half-days without the relevant period) render as inert drop targets: hatched --hairline wash, no-drop cursor, drag ghost dims.
  • On an attempted drop, a small inline affordance offers "Move to next school day →" — one tap accepts; anything else cancels. No modal.
  • "Next instructional day" is computed from the configured school week + holiday calendar + rotation cycle — the lesson lands on the next day where its subject actually meets.
  • Same logic guards programmatic moves: catch-up reflow and bulk shift (06) skip non-instructional days automatically.
  • Every assisted move fires an undo toast (02) naming the landing day.

Surfaces affected

Weekly · drag layer Daily · reorder Catch-up · reflow

Acceptance criteria

  • No gesture can ever schedule a lesson on a non-instructional day.
  • The assist correctly accounts for custom school weeks, holidays, and A/B rotation.
  • Inert-day treatment passes AA contrast and is distinguishable without color alone.

Depends on: holidays rendering on Weekly/Daily (Phase 1B) and the rotation-cycle engine. Undo toast (02) is a prerequisite for the assist UX.

06 · Catch-up gesture

Bulk shift — move many lessons at once

P2Phase 1B

Select a range of lessons — or a whole subject-week — and push everything forward N instructional days. This is the real-world catch-up gesture; doing it card-by-card is painful.

Design spec

  • Entry: a "Select" mode from the Weekly toolbar and from Catch-up. Cards gain quiet checkboxes; shift-click selects a range; the subject lane header selects its whole week.
  • A bottom action bar (replacing the toast position while active) shows the count and one primary action: "Shift → next school day", with a stepper for N days.
  • Shift counts instructional days per the school week + holidays + rotation — never calendar days.
  • Preview before commit: target days highlight in the grid as the stepper changes, so the teacher sees the landing pattern before confirming.
  • In Personal mode a bulk shift forks moved lessons lazily (move-tier stripe); in Master mode it follows the banner-gated flow. One undo toast covers the whole batch.
  • Phone: selection via long-press; action bar is thumb-reachable, ≥44px targets.

Surfaces affected

Weekly · toolbar + grid Catch-up · triage

Acceptance criteria

  • A teacher can move a full subject-week forward one school day in ≤4 interactions.
  • A single undo reverses the entire batch.
  • Shifted lessons show the correct move-tier visual (solid stripe + move arrow) per lesson.

Depends on: holiday-aware scheduling (05) and undo toasts (02). Catch-up rules in Settings define the default N.

07 · Collaboration

Deep links to every week, unit, and lesson

P2Phase 1A.5

A teacher should be able to paste "look at Week 14 math" into the team chat. Mostly routing discipline, big collaboration payoff.

Design spec

  • URL scheme extends existing routes: /weekly?week=14&subject=math, /daily?date=2026-09-14&lesson=…, /subject/math#unit-3. Grade scoping is always a parameter, never assumed.
  • Opening a lesson deep link lands on the right view with the lesson detail open and the card brought into view (container scroll).
  • A "Copy link" action in every lesson/unit overflow menu and in the lesson detail header; confirmation via the toast (02): "Link copied".
  • Links resolve in the viewer's own mode: Personal-first viewing means I see my version of your lesson — with the divergence stripe telling me if ours differ. The diff view (01) is one click away.
  • State worth sharing goes in the URL; ephemeral state (open menus, selection) does not.

Surfaces affected

Routing · all views Card + detail menus

Acceptance criteria

  • Every week, unit, and lesson has a stable URL that survives refresh and restores view state.
  • Copy-link is reachable in ≤2 clicks from any lesson card.
  • Links shared between teachers respect each viewer's Personal-first resolution.

Depends on: nothing for routing; auth-gated resolution arrives with Supabase. Do the URL scheme now — the command palette (04) builds on it.

08 · Perceived performance

Skeleton states matching card geometry

P21B gate

When Supabase lands, loading should paint the Weekly grid's exact card shapes in subject-surface tints — not spinners. Cheap to spec now, painful to retrofit.

Design spec

  • One skeleton per canonical primitive: Weekly card, Daily lesson row, Year roadmap bar, Subject unit card. Each matches its real component's dimensions, radius, and left-stripe slot exactly — no layout shift on data arrival.
  • Fill is neutral --idle-tint with a slow shimmer; where the subject is already known from route context (e.g. a subject page), the skeleton uses that subject's --c-surface so color-as-navigation holds even while loading.
  • Shimmer disabled under prefers-reduced-motion — static tint only.
  • Counts mirror reality: the Weekly skeleton paints the configured school-week columns and typical per-day card counts, not a generic 5×3.
  • Spinners are reserved for in-place actions (a saving button); never for view loads.

Surfaces affected

Weekly Daily Year Subject

Acceptance criteria

  • Zero cumulative layout shift between skeleton and loaded state on the four primary views.
  • Skeleton column count derives from the configured school week.
  • No spinner appears on any full-view load path.

Depends on: nothing to design; only matters once real network latency exists. Spec it as part of each primitive in components/ui/ before the 1B wiring starts.

09 · Ambient awareness

Quiet team signals

P3Phase 1B+

Tiny avatar dots on a lesson showing which teammates forked or completed it. No feed, no notifications — just ambient awareness that supports the team-planning story.

Design spec

  • On the lesson detail (not the grid card — the grid stays clean): a row of ≤6 dawn-gradient initial avatars, 18px, with state — solid ring = completed, dashed ring = forked. Hover/long-press names the teacher and state.
  • Master-mode view of a lesson may show an aggregate line: "3 of 5 teachers completed · 2 forked" — a planning signal for the team lead, phrased as information, never as pressure.
  • Hard restraint: no counts on grid cards, no feed, no push notifications, no "activity" tab. If a signal needs attention, it belongs in Catch-up.
  • Per-school setting to disable team signals entirely (some teams won't want visibility of pace differences).

Surfaces affected

Daily · lesson detail Master mode · lesson detail

Acceptance criteria

  • Grid cards remain visually unchanged — signals live in the detail surface only.
  • States update via realtime subscription without reload.
  • The per-school off switch removes every signal surface, including the aggregate line.

Depends on: Supabase realtime + multi-teacher rows — strictly after the 1B wave is stable. Lowest urgency on this list; highest sensitivity. Validate with the beta team before building.
