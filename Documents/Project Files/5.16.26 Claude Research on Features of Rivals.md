# Competitive Landscape Analysis: Curriculum Planning Web App for International School Teachers

*Prepared May 2026 — for a planning tool targeting US, UK, Europe, IB and international school markets, priced at ~$18/teacher/year*

---

## Executive Summary

The curriculum-planning market splits cleanly into four bands:

1. **Cheap individual-teacher planners** (Planbook.com $20/yr, Planboard/Chalk free, PlanbookEdu $25/yr, Common Curriculum free/$4.89 mo). Strong on weekly grids, weak on team curriculum and weak on international frameworks.
2. **Enterprise curriculum-mapping platforms** sold per-school (Atlas, ManageBac+, Toddle, Curriculum Trak). Strong on unit planning, standards mapping, accreditation and IB workflows; weak on individual-teacher daily UX; opaque, school-only pricing.
3. **AI productivity tools** (MagicSchool $99.96/yr, Brisk Teaching free–$99/yr Pro, Diffit $14.99/mo, Curipod, Eduaide, SchoolAI, Oak's Aila). These generate content but do not host a curriculum.
4. **Resource/collaboration surfaces** used as ad-hoc curriculum tools (Padlet $199/yr Classroom, Wakelet, BookWidgets).

Your proposed app sits in a deliberate gap: **the affordable, team-collaborative, multi-framework, teacher-first daily planner that respects both the master curriculum (institutional) and the personal planbook (individual)**. Nobody combines (a) Git-style master/personal forking, (b) a graduated Simple→Task→Advanced UX, (c) a year-round "catch-up" reconciliation view, and (d) sub-$20/teacher pricing in one product.

## Section 1 — Niche Analysis (Comparison & Differentiation)

### 1. Planbook.com

- **What they do well:** The dominant individual-teacher daily/weekly planner in the US. Flat $20/yr, one tier, 90-day free trial, all features unlocked. 1M+ teachers, 1B+ lessons. Account types: Teacher, Administrator, District, Dual, Manager (free), Student. Rotating, A/B and weekday schedules; standards attachment; admin view/approve workflows; view links for parents/subs.
- **Pricing:** Flat $20/teacher/year, school discounts.
- **Target:** US K-12 public school teachers; thin internationally.
- **Similarities:** Per-teacher annual pricing, weekly/daily planner, standards attachment, AB-week schedules, sharing, year-to-year copying.
- **Differences:** No Git-style fork — collaboration is invitation-based to a single planbook. Standards are largely US Common Core; IB/Cambridge/NZC thin. No Catch-up screen, no Simple/Task/Advanced toggle, no AI, no Teaching Reminders, no "personal subjects with promotion."
- **Niche gap your app fills:** A truly *team-first* curriculum object (Master) that still gives the individual teacher a private working copy (Personal) without copy-paste or accidental overwriting.

### 2. Toddle

- **What they do well:** The most polished IB-focused curriculum + LMS platform. PYP/MYP/DP/CP coverage; Cambridge, English NC, Australian/NZ curricula, UbD, bespoke frameworks. Deeply embedded "Toddle AI" for unit planning, report cards, criterion-based MYP grading, AI tutors (Plato). Pre-loaded CIS/NEASC/WASC/KHDA/ADEK accreditation dashboards. 50+ integrations including Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Veracross, Blackbaud, PowerSchool, Turnitin, Clever. 100,000+ educators across 2,500+ schools in 100+ countries.
- **Pricing:** Three institutional tiers — Planning Pro, Essentials, Ultimate — all custom-quoted on whole-school adoption with multi-year discounts. Free 2–3 week trial. Not publicly listed.
- **Target:** IB World Schools, international schools, IB candidates, UbD/progressive independents.
- **Similarities:** Multi-framework standards, team curriculum, resource attachments, mobile, AB-cycle scheduling, family communication.
- **Differences:** Toddle is a *whole-school replacement platform*. No Master/Personal forking; no Simple/Task/Advanced lenses; no Catch-up screen; AI is generative-first whereas your Teaching Reminders is curated-research-first.
- **Niche gap your app fills:** Toddle costs schools tens of thousands; your $18/teacher hits teachers and small departments who can't get a school PO. Master/Personal fork is also structurally absent.

### 3. Common Curriculum (Cc / "Common Planner")

- **What they do well:** Free Basic tier with daily/weekly/monthly planning, standards search, Google Classroom posting, lesson re-use, Drive/Dropbox/Office 365 uploads. Pro ($4.89/mo annual ≈ $59/yr) adds unit planning, automatic standards tracking, class website, 5 collaborators. School ($90/teacher/year) adds team org, template sharing, school-wide collaboration, free training. AI Lesson Assistant added recently with tiered usage.
- **Pricing:** Free / $4.89 mo / $90/teacher/year.
- **Target:** US K-12, small charter/independent.
- **Similarities:** Weekly grid, unit plan, standards, file attachments, collaboration, AI.
- **Differences:** Co-editing only, no fork pattern. Standards are CCSS + state. No Catch-up, no three-mode UX, no A/B-week, no image-stack attachments.
- **Niche gap your app fills:** International standards breadth, richer team model, and a price point well below their $90 school tier.

### 4. Atlas (Faria Atlas, formerly Rubicon Atlas / OnAtlas)

- **What they do well:** The dominant *curriculum-mapping* platform for independent and international schools — 6,000+ schools. Scope-and-sequence reports, unit templates, 600+ standards, public curriculum websites, gap/redundancy analytics, integrates with Canvas/Blackboard/Google Classroom. Migrating Curriculum Trak schools onto Atlas, 2025–2028.
- **Pricing:** Custom institutional quotes only.
- **Target:** Independent, international, and (via Rubicon channel) US public schools serious about curriculum mapping. Sister to ManageBac under Faria.
- **Similarities:** Multi-framework standards, unit/lesson hierarchy, team curriculum, coverage analytics, templates.
- **Differences:** Heavyweight, admin-led — teachers experience it as reporting, not daily planning. No daily/weekly grid in the planner sense, no Catch-up, no Simple/Task/Advanced lens, no Personal copies (curriculum is institutional).
- **Niche gap your app fills:** Curriculum-mapping-flavored *daily planner* at a teacher-affordable price.

### 5. ManageBac+ (Faria)

- **What they do well:** The original IB-dedicated platform, trusted by 2,300+ DP and 1,000+ MYP schools, and 3,000+ international schools overall. Only platform fully integrated with **IBIS** (IB Information System) for exam registration, e-coursework, CAS moderation. Now ManageBac+ with British International Schools mode (new 2025), AI Writing Assistant, AssessPrep, Report Cards, Zoom/KeyChat, Faria SpotLight cross-platform analytics.
- **Pricing:** Annual subscription per total students, custom-quoted. ISO 27001, GDPR compliant.
- **Target:** IB schools first; expanding to British International, IGCSE, AP, multi-curricula.
- **Similarities:** Curriculum planning, assessment, multi-framework, mobile.
- **Differences:** Institutional pricing, no individual option, no fork pattern, no Catch-up, no UX-mode toggle. The IBIS integration is a moat your app should *not* try to cross — let ManageBac own it.
- **Niche gap your app fills:** The non-IB-flagship daily workflow inside IB schools, where teachers still keep their working plan in Google Docs because ManageBac feels admin-facing.

### 6. Curriculum Trak

Historically the curriculum-mapping platform for faith-based US Christian schools. Acquired by Faria; all schools migrating to Atlas Spring 2025–2028 in 2–4 week migration windows. Custom institutional pricing transitioning to Atlas's quote model. **Effectively defunct as a standalone competitor**; no need to design against it.

### 7. Chalk.com (Planboard)

- **What they do well:** Free for individual teachers — weekly planner with templates, standards integration (US state, Canadian provincial, UK, international), Google Classroom integration, rich media, color-coded subjects, native iOS/iPad/Android apps, A/B rotating schedules, copy-and-shift lessons, PDF print. 250,000+ teachers globally. Markboard (standards-based gradebook), Attendance, Collaborate as adjacent products.
- **Pricing:** Free for individuals. Chalk Gold $9/mo or $99/year. School pricing on request.
- **Target:** Individual K-12 teachers globally, particularly Canada and US.
- **Similarities:** Big overlap on weekly planner, AB schedules, rich-media attachment, standards, mobile.
- **Differences:** No Master/Personal fork (sharing is direct collaboration). No Catch-up. No three-mode UX. No personal-subjects promotion. No bulk master-change approval. No right-click framework-scoped tagging.
- **Niche gap your app fills:** Stronger team curriculum model; deeper international framework support; pedagogical scaffolding via Teaching Reminders and intervention strategies.

### 8. MagicSchool (AI productivity — adjacent)

- **What they do well:** Most widely adopted AI assistant for teachers in 2025–26. 60+ tools: lesson plan, worksheet, IEP, rubric, text leveler, translator, AI-resistant assignment, YouTube questions, Raina chatbot, presentation maker, Student Rooms. SOC 2 / FERPA / COPPA. LMS integration with Clever, ClassLink, Canvas, Schoology. Custom-tool builder and district document upload at Enterprise tier.
- **Pricing:** Free (5-gen history, monthly limits) / Plus $12.99 mo or $99.96/year ($8.33/mo annual) / Enterprise custom-quoted.
- **Target:** US K-12 teachers and districts; international growing.
- **Relationship to your app:** Not a curriculum host — a content generator. Your app is the *home* lesson plans land in. The integration relationship (Phase 3) is more promising than head-to-head competition.

### 9. Brisk Teaching (AI Chrome extension — adjacent)

- **What they do well:** Chrome/Edge extension inside Google Docs, Slides, Classroom, Gmail, YouTube. 30+ AI tools: targeted feedback, presentation maker, podcast generator, IEP/504/MTSS generators, AI writing detection. Free with 20+ tools and usage limits; Educator Pro ~$99.99/year for advanced feedback styles and Docs comment embedding. District plan adds **Brisk Next** ("curriculum-grounded AI") and **Brisk Boost** (student-safe AI workspace). FERPA-compliant. Desktop-only — no mobile.
- **Pricing:** Free / Pro $99.99 yr / district custom.
- **Target:** Teachers in Google Workspace.
- **Relationship to your app:** Brisk's "Curriculum Intelligence" feature (grounding AI in the school's own curriculum) is the model your Phase-3 AI differentiation should adopt. Brisk needs a curriculum source of truth; you *are* one — partnership potential.

### 10. Padlet

- **What they do well:** Visual bulletin-board for any media — text, images, videos, links, files up to 1GB (Classroom+), live collaboration, AI auto-moderation (Safety Net), AI image generation, LMS integration via LTI 1.3 (School tier), 38+ language UI. Wall/grid/canvas/map/timeline/list/stream layouts. The reference your spec cites for "resource boards used by teachers as a curriculum surface."
- **Pricing:** Free (3 padlets, 20MB cap); Gold $6.99 mo; Platinum $9.99 mo; Team from $14.99 mo; Classroom $199/year (2 teachers, 100 students); School from $1,000/year for 10 teachers.
- **Target:** Teachers as the largest segment.
- **Similarities:** Your resource-attachment spec is consciously Padlet-inspired — PDFs/DOCX/RTF/images/image-stack slideshows + link previews, 25 MB/file, 10 hosted/lesson, unlimited links on Cloudflare R2.
- **Differences:** Padlet has no curriculum, standards, or schedule. You apply Padlet ergonomics to a *structured* curriculum object.
- **Niche gap your app fills:** "Padlet for the curriculum row" — keep the tactile media joy, but inside a planned lesson event.

### 11. Oak National Academy + Aila (UK; not a planner, but reshaping UK expectations)

- **What they do well:** Free, fully-resourced, openly-licensed UK National Curriculum lessons across 14 subjects, KS1–KS4. 10,000+ lessons, slides, worksheets, quizzes. **Aila** (launched September 2024) is a GPT-4o-based AI lesson assistant grounded in Oak's curriculum and NC England. Trial evidence: ~3.5 hours/week saved on planning. UK DfE arm's-length body; £43M curriculum + £2M AI funding. Curriculum data published openly with an MCP server.
- **Pricing:** Free at point of use.
- **Target:** UK state schools — Reception to Year 11.
- **Implication for your app:** Not a planner — a content provider. But every UK teacher will compare your standards integration to Oak's, and Aila is now the free baseline UK teachers measure AI against. Your wedge: a *team* planning surface (Oak is per-resource, not per-team) that travels across schools and countries. Phase 4 opportunity: import Oak curriculum data via its open MCP server.

### 12. AI Lesson-Content Generators (Diffit, Curipod, Eduaide, SchoolAI, Twee, Khanmigo, Edcafe)

Brief assessments — none are direct planner competitors, but together they define what "AI for teachers" means today:

- **Diffit** — differentiates text across reading levels and languages; ~$14.99/mo paid, generous free tier; cited-source attribution. Strong for ELL/SEN.
- **Curipod** — generates interactive slide decks with polls/AI feedback; free + Schools plan (custom).
- **Eduaide.ai** — 150+ resource templates, grounded in pedagogical frameworks (5E, UbD, Gagné, UDL); free trial, ~$5.99/mo and up.
- **SchoolAI** — student-facing AI "Spaces" with teacher monitoring; 200,000+ reusable Spaces.
- **Twee** — language-teaching AI generator (questions, dialogues from YouTube/text); language teacher niche.
- **Khanmigo** — Khan Academy's AI tutor; free for US teachers since 2024 (US-only as of 2026). Tutor-first, not planner.
- **Edcafe AI** — newer entrant emphasising full-cycle workflow (create → assign → grade → track), with claimed 29% engagement uplift in a 2025 peer-reviewed study; free 100 generations/mo, Pro $7.99/mo.

**Implication:** AI is now table-stakes for content generation. Your differentiation is *not* to out-build MagicSchool — it's to (a) be the structured home where AI outputs land and persist, (b) ground AI in the school's actual Master curriculum, and (c) keep AI Phase-3, not Phase-1 distraction.

### 13. Australasian stack — Compass + Edrolo + Sentral

- **Compass Education** — dominant Australian school management platform; 5,000+ schools globally, 2M+ AU parents. SIS + attendance + billing + learning tasks + semester reports + parent app. Integrates with Toddle, Hapara/Google Classroom, Canvas, SchoolBox, Edrolo. DoE-approved in NSW, VIC, QLD, TAS, WA.
- **Edrolo** — Years 7–12 curriculum-aligned content for NESA, VCAA, QCAA, ACv9. 1,000+ schools, 17,000+ educators, 200,000+ students. Cites 30% lift in top results for students doing ≥10 questions/week for ≥10 weeks.
- **Sentral** — Australian SIS competitor to Compass; integrates with Toddle.

**Implication:** AU/NZ proper is dominated by SIS-led ecosystems with Toddle layered on. Your wedge in Australasia is international schools in APAC (Singapore, Hong Kong, Bangkok, Tokyo) rather than mainland AU/NZ public schools.

### 14. UK adaptive-learning ecosystem (Century Tech, Sparx)

- **Century Tech** — AI-driven adaptive learning paths for students (UK schools, English/Maths/Science). Sold per-school. Not a planner; not a planning competitor.
- **Sparx Maths / Sparx Reader** — homework practice platforms; school subscriptions. Not a planner.

UK schools with these tools already feel "tooled up." Your value is the teacher's planner that respects the National Curriculum and exam-board structure, with Oak resources attachable as links.

### 15. Resource curation and worksheet tools

- **Wakelet** — collection-and-share tool focused on linear content presentation (Padlet alternative). Free for individual teachers; Spaces and pro features on paid plans. Useful for shareable resource bundles but not a planner.
- **BookWidgets** — interactive worksheet/quiz builder used heavily in Belgium, Netherlands, UK. Teacher subscription (~$99/yr) with deep activity types. Not a planner, but a likely *integration target* — teachers will want to embed BookWidgets activities in lesson events.

### 16. European platforms (German market)

- **IServ** — dominant Germany school portal: messaging, file sharing, email, calendar, video conferencing, simple gradebook. Closed/on-premise feel. Not a curriculum planner; you may need IServ link-preview support to enter the German market.
- **schul.cloud** — German secure-messaging-and-collaboration platform for schools (Heinekingmedia). Primarily messaging/files; not curriculum planning.
- **Implication:** German market has no dominant curriculum *planner*; the gap is genuine but the buying process is regional/Länder-led and slow. Better Phase-3 expansion target than launch focus.

### 17. Tools that came up but are *not* meaningful competitors

- **Schoology / Canvas / Blackboard / Google Classroom** — LMS, not curriculum planners. They are *integration targets*, not competitors. (Brisk, MagicSchool, Toddle all integrate with them.)
- **Newsela** — content provider (leveled articles); not a planner; potential partner.
- **Khan Academy Districts** — student-facing learning platform; not a planner.
- **Lessonly** — corporate learning/training tool, not K-12; out of scope.
- **Build Your Stack / BuildYourStack** — an NCTE-led literacy *movement* and stack-sharing format (not a software product); not a competitor.
- **Eduflow** — higher-ed/professional learning LMS, now part of Multiverse; not a K-12 planner.
- **Subject (subject.com)** — secondary-school video-based supplemental curriculum (US, primarily English Language Arts); a content provider, not a planning tool. May surface in school content-budget comparisons but does not collide with your planning surface.
- **Pondr / Pondr.io** — research returned no significant K-12 curriculum-planning footprint at the depth needed to evaluate. Flag as a low-priority watch item.

### Niche Your App Uniquely Fills

> **The team-collaborative, multi-framework, daily-and-yearly planner for the individual teacher and small department — at a price the teacher can swipe a personal credit card for.**

No competitor combines:
1. **Master/Personal fork with three-tier visual differentiation** — Atlas/Toddle/ManageBac have institutional curriculum but no personal overlay; Planbook/Common Curriculum/Planboard have personal plans but no master-team object.
2. **Simple / Task / Advanced UX lenses** — every competitor has a single density. Toddle's role-based views are permission slices, not opt-in cognitive-load lenses for the same user.
3. **Catch-up screen** — no competitor surfaces an annual reconciliation of uncovered events with bulk carry-over destination picker. Structurally novel.
4. **Teaching Reminders banner** — curated, *non-generative* pedagogical content surface (vs. AI generation) is contrarian and unique.
5. **Personal subjects with promotion-to-team** — recognizes classroom routines (Morning Meeting, Circle Time) as real curriculum. No competitor models this.
6. **~$18/teacher/year** — undercuts Planbook ($20), Common Curriculum Schools ($90), Chalk Gold ($99), Brisk Pro ($99.99), MagicSchool Plus ($99.96), Padlet Classroom (~$100/teacher), and dwarfs per-teacher Toddle/Atlas/ManageBac institutional deals.

## Section 3 — Feature Prioritization for Your Roadmap

Each item names *why* and *which competitor(s)* set the expectation.

### Phase 1 — Beta launch (May–August 2026): minimum competitive surface

1. **Master/Personal fork with three-tier visual differentiation** — your headline differentiator with master-mode safety banner (flash → persistent red). *Nobody has this; it must be the story.*
2. **Weekly grid + daily view + unit view** — table stakes. Planbook, Common Curriculum, Planboard, Toddle.
3. **AB-week scheduling** — required for secondary international and UK timetables. Planbook and Chalk both have it.
4. **CCSS preloaded + at least 3 international frameworks at launch (IB MYP/DP, English NC, Cambridge)** — your "not just a US tool" credential. Toddle and ManageBac+ lead with this; Planbook and Common Curriculum fail it.
5. **Three event types: Core Lesson Events, Extra Lesson Events (recurring allowed), Day Events** — model real teacher reality. Toddle and Common Curriculum collapse this less cleanly; clarity is the win.
6. **Resource attachments — PDF, DOCX, RTF, images, image stacks, link previews for web/YouTube/Google Drive** — 25 MB/file, 10 hosted files/lesson, unlimited links. Padlet sets the tactile bar; Common Curriculum and Chalk meet the link/file basics.
7. **Print Center (configurable, scope chips, year-overview)** — admins and substitutes want PDFs. Planbook, Common Curriculum, Chalk, Toddle all print well.
8. **Google Classroom posting + Google Drive link preview + Microsoft 365 file open** — every competitor has this; skipping loses Workspace-for-Education and Microsoft schools instantly.
9. **Simple/Task/Advanced UX mode toggle** — ship at least Simple + Advanced; Task can tighten post-launch. Your second headline differentiator, and the answer to "Toddle feels complex."
10. **Per-teacher pricing page with $18/year published transparently** — Toddle/Atlas/ManageBac+/Curriculum Trak all hide pricing behind sales calls. Self-serve and cheap is itself a competitive feature.
11. **Auth, SSO basics (Google, Microsoft), GDPR posture page, FERPA/COPPA documentation** — international and EU schools will ask; MagicSchool, Toddle, Brisk all publish posture docs.
12. **Mobile-responsive web (no native app required for v1)** — Chalk has native and it's a strength, but Toddle works in browser and so does Planbook. Native is Phase 2.

### Phase 2 — 3–6 months post-launch: closing competitor gaps

13. **Catch-up screen** — full-page reconciliation of uncovered events, scope chips, group-by, bulk actions, carry-over destination picker. Third headline differentiator. *No competitor has it; promote heavily.*
14. **Teaching Reminders banner with behavioral/academic toggle** — research-backed quotes/summaries. Differentiator vs. the "more AI noise" of MagicSchool/Brisk. Frame as evidence-based pedagogy.
15. **Personal subjects with promotion-to-team flow + lead approval** — Morning Meeting / Circle Time use case. Toddle's strict unit hierarchy can't model this; lead with it for primary schools.
16. **Subject Team Membership / per-subject editing permissions** — match Toddle and Atlas team controls. Required at international school scale.
17. **Recurring learning events inside units + recurring extra lesson events** — daily rituals. Chalk and Planbook handle simple recurrence; you differentiate by allowing recurrence *inside* a unit.
18. **Bulk master-change approval (review screen, Accept/Reject all, conflict flagging)** — enables team-lead workflow Toddle does only through admin permissions. Bar set by Faria/Atlas analytics.
19. **Additional standards frameworks: NZ Curriculum, Australian Curriculum v9, school-uploaded custom frameworks** — opens AU/NZ and bespoke independents. Toddle has both; Atlas has both.
20. **Quick right-click standards tagging filtered to subject's default frameworks** — feels modern; comparable to Common Curriculum's automatic tracking but faster.
21. **Year-end Rollover Decision screen (opt-in per personal subject; "ask me at rollover" default)** — your unique anti-data-loss feature; no competitor structures this.
22. **Native iOS/Android apps** — Chalk and Toddle have them; without them, teachers will pull out phones at standing meetings and find you missing.
23. **AI integration (light) — auto-suggest learning objectives, generate lesson narrative from bullet points, parent-friendly summary** — table stakes; Toddle, MagicSchool, Brisk, Common Curriculum, ManageBac+, Aila all have it. Phase-2 minimum: a "Generate" button with 3–4 useful prompts. Position as "AI inside your curriculum," not "AI outside."
24. **Class website / public view-link** — Common Curriculum and Planbook both let parents/students see a static class page. Cheap to add, high optics.

### Phase 3 — 12–18 months post-launch: strategic differentiators

25. **Tier 2-3 intervention strategies attached to learning events** — AI-suggested differentiation grounded in the lesson's standards and the student's needs. Bar set by Brisk's MTSS Intervention Creator and MagicSchool's IEP tools; *your* angle is that strategies live attached to the curriculum row, not in a separate document. Big win for SEN/inclusion leads.
26. **Permissions Admin page — roster + per-teacher profile with permission matrix** — required for school-tier deals. Toddle, Atlas, ManageBac+ all have it.
27. **Resource annotation (PDF mark-up, image annotation)** — your Phase-3 commitment. Padlet doesn't really do this; BookWidgets and Kami do.
28. **Curriculum-grounded AI (Brisk's Curriculum Intelligence model)** — when "Generate worksheet" runs, the AI uses *this school's Master curriculum and standards* as context. In 18 months this becomes a competitive necessity.
29. **Accreditation evidence tagging (CIS / NEASC / WASC / KHDA / IB Evaluation)** — to be a credible alternative to ManageBac+/Toddle, you need the documentation surface even without full dashboards. Faria has it preloaded; IB schools will ask.
30. **LTI 1.3 / Common Cartridge integration (Canvas, Schoology, Blackboard)** — Padlet's School tier uses LTI 1.3; Toddle integrates with 50+. Crosses the threshold from "consumer" to "institutional."
31. **SIS rostering (PowerSchool, Veracross, Blackbaud, Compass, Sentral)** — opens AU/NZ and US independents. ManageBac+ and Toddle compete on rostering depth.
32. **Standards-coverage analytics for admins / heads of department** — Atlas's core moat. You don't need their depth — just "uncovered" and "duplicated" standards reports.
33. **Parent-facing share view (per-student, per-week) with privacy controls** — matches Common Curriculum's auto-updating class website without giving up the planner UI.
34. **Multi-language UI (start: English, Spanish, French, German, Simplified Chinese)** — ManageBac+ is in 4 languages for teachers, 7 for parents; Padlet 38+. Even 3 visibly improves EU/Latin America/Asia conversion.
35. **Lesson observation / walkthrough mode for admins** — Planbook's admin view is light, Toddle's is deep; mid-depth is enough.

### Phase 4 — Aspirational features (post-18 months)

Things competitors don't have or do poorly. Each is a candidate "category-redefining" move.

36. **"Curriculum diff" view** — borrowed from your Git inspiration. Show the teacher exactly what changed between Master and Personal at a glance, line-level highlight. *Nobody in K-12 does this; natural extension of the fork architecture.*
37. **Cross-school Master curriculum marketplace / template store** — paid teachers publish a Master (e.g., "Year 6 Cambridge English 2026") for other schools to fork. Atlas has nothing like this; TpT/Share My Lesson are unstructured marketplaces.
38. **Curriculum versioning & branch history** — show a Master's revision history; allow revert to an earlier version. Trivial given the fork architecture, impossible in any current competitor.
39. **AI catch-up suggestions** — when the Catch-up screen has 14 uncovered events, AI proposes 3 carry-over plans (compressed / extended / mid-year skipped). Combines your unique Catch-up surface with the AI capability competitors race on.
40. **Wellbeing / cognitive-load nudges** — extend Teaching Reminders to personalized "you've stacked 3 high-load lessons this week; consider shuffling." No competitor approaches teacher wellbeing through plan structure.
41. **Open-curriculum import from Oak National Academy** — Oak publishes data openly with an MCP server. Be the first paid planner to ingest Oak units as forkable Masters. A free moat for the UK market.
42. **Native gradebook + standards-based grading** — Chalk has Markboard; Toddle/ManageBac+ have full gradebooks. Adds "small-school all-in-one" positioning with revenue upside but scope risk.
43. **Substitute-teacher mode** — one-click "share today's plan with a sub" that hides personal notes and exposes only what's needed. Planbook has view-links; nobody has a true sub-mode.
44. **Curriculum-to-portfolio linkage** — generate student-facing portfolio entries auto-tagged to the curriculum row that produced them. Toddle's portfolios are best-in-class; this is table stakes in 24 months.
45. **API for integration partners (Brisk, MagicSchool, Diffit, BookWidgets)** — let third-party AI tools query your curriculum as the source of truth. Turns competitors into distribution partners, mirroring Faria inside its own product family.

## Risk Notes and Caveats

- **AI is a moving target.** Pricing and feature claims for MagicSchool, Brisk, Diffit, Curipod, Eduaide, SchoolAI, Oak's Aila and Toddle AI were accurate as of 2025–early 2026 vendor pages, but this category iterates every 6 months. Treat any AI feature in your Phase-3 roadmap as a directional capability, not a fixed surface.
- **"Curriculum-First" is a contested phrase.** Faria/Atlas/ManageBac+ have used "Curriculum-First Learning Platform" since at least 2018. Position with a different anchor phrase (e.g., "Curriculum-Forked Planner") to avoid being absorbed into their established narrative.
- **Pricing transparency is your weapon.** Toddle, ManageBac+, Atlas, Curriculum Trak are all custom-quote only. Your published $18/teacher/year is unusually visible in this market and is itself a competitive feature — protect it.
- **Some competitors not deeply verified.** Pondr, Subject (subject.com), IServ, schul.cloud, BookWidgets, Wakelet, Century Tech, Sparx did not surface in primary vendor-page detail during this research pass. Treat the brief mentions as flagged areas to validate before any go-to-market focus on UK, German, or EU markets. Curriculum Trak is effectively being absorbed into Atlas and can be deprioritized.
- **AU/NZ proper belongs to Compass + Sentral + Toddle + Edrolo.** Entering AU/NZ public schools will be uphill. International schools across APAC (Singapore, Hong Kong, Bangkok, Tokyo) are the better wedge for your IB/Cambridge international play.
- **Biggest single threat: Toddle going downmarket.** Toddle's published institutional pricing is custom and tied to whole-school adoption, but its depth and trust in 2,500+ schools means a self-serve "Toddle for Teachers" tier — should they launch one — would be the most credible threat to your wedge. Build the Master/Personal fork moat *before* they decide to.
