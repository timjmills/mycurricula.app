# MyCurricula — Onboarding & Lesson-Flow Template Plan

> **Status:** Plan v1 — 2026-05-17. Research-backed proposal for review before build.
> **Inputs:** competitor onboarding research (commonplanner.com, Planbook, Planboard,
> Toddle), the design-handoff screenshots, the planning document (§4 data model, §5.10
> Settings, §6 first-run notes), and `CLAUDE.md`.
> **Supersedes:** the thin 3-step welcome modal described in planning doc §6.

---

## 1. Goals & principles

The app cannot be used until a teacher has told it: what grade, which days the school
runs, how the schedule rotates, what subjects they teach, and (for teaching subjects)
how a lesson is structured. Today none of this has a setup flow — it is scattered
across Settings. This plan defines a **first-run onboarding wizard** and a new
**lesson-flow template system**.

**Principles**

1. **As simple and step-by-step as possible.** One decision per screen. No screen asks
   two unrelated things. A teacher can finish in under 10 minutes.
2. **Smart defaults, always editable.** Every screen is pre-filled with a sensible
   default (Grade 5, the 8 known subjects, Gradual-Release template). The teacher
   confirms or tweaks — they never face a blank form.
3. **Skippable where safe.** Standards and the daily schedule can be deferred to
   Settings; grade, school week, and subjects cannot.
4. **Show, don't tell.** Color swatches, template section previews, and a live summary
   — the teacher sees what they are choosing.
5. **Nothing hard-coded.** The school week and schedule cycle chosen here drive every
   calendar surface (see `CLAUDE.md` §1, §6). Onboarding is the source of that config.
6. **Resumable.** Progress is saved per step; closing the browser resumes where it
   left off.

## 2. The onboarding wizard — step by step

A linear wizard: progress bar at top, **Back** / **Continue** at bottom, one job per
screen. Steps 6 and 7 show a **Skip for now** link.

### Step 0 — Welcome
- Confirm display name (pre-filled from Google SSO).
- One sentence on what's about to happen: "Let's set up your planner — about 8 minutes."
- **Continue.**

### Step 1 — Grade level
- "What grade do you teach?" — a grid of grade chips (K, 1–12, **Multiple grades**).
- Grade 5 pre-selected for the beta. Drives standards defaults and template suggestion.
- *Backed by:* `GradeLevel` / `TeacherGradeAssignment`.

### Step 2 — Your school week
- "Which days is your school open?" — two preset buttons (**Sunday–Thursday**,
  **Monday–Friday**) plus **Custom**.
- Custom reveals a 7-day toggle row (Sun…Sat) — supports a 3-day week or any subset.
- Below: school-year start & end date pickers (defaults to the current year).
- *Backed by:* new `SchoolWeekConfig` (see §5). This is the field the data model is
  currently missing.

### Step 3 — Schedule rotation
- "Does your schedule repeat the same way every week?"
  - **Yes — same every week** (default; done).
  - **A/B rotation** — alternating day patterns; set which day the cycle starts.
  - **Numbered cycle** — Day 1…Day N rotating independent of the week; enter N
    (2–20) and the cycle's anchor date. Covers "A/B every 4 days," 6-day rotations,
    etc. (`CLAUDE.md` §1).
- Plain-language helper text; most teachers pick "same every week" and move on.
- *Backed by:* `ScheduleCycle` fields on `SchoolYear` (`cycle_type`, `cycle_length`,
  `cycle_anchor_date`) — partially in the spec as `active_cycle_pattern`; generalized
  here.

### Step 4 — Your subjects  ← the heart of onboarding
The screen lists subject rows. It is **pre-filled with the 8 Grade 5 subjects** (Math,
Reading, Writing, Grammar, Spelling, UFLI, Explorers, SEL) so the teacher mostly just
confirms. Each row, top to bottom, is one subject and exposes **three controls**:

1. **Name** — editable text.
2. **Color** — a swatch that opens the palette picker (the 20-color paired palette from
   the design system). This is where each subject's color is chosen. The chosen
   mapping becomes the team-wide subject→color mapping; Appearance settings can adjust
   it later.
3. **Type — Academic or Non-academic** — a two-state toggle:
   - **Academic** (default for teaching subjects): lessons in this subject use the
     **lesson-flow template** (§3) — structured sections, objectives, standards.
   - **Non-academic** (lunch, recess, morning meeting, assembly, PE if not planned):
     lessons/blocks are a plain title + note, **no lesson-flow template, no sections,
     no standards**. Keeps the planner uncluttered for blocks that aren't taught.

Affordances: **+ Add subject**, drag-to-reorder, remove. Adding a subject picks the
next unused palette color automatically. Reorder sets `display_order`.

*Backed by:* `Subject` gains `color` and `is_academic` (see §5).

### Step 5 — Default lesson structure
- Only shown if at least one subject is **Academic**.
- "How do you usually structure a lesson?" — a gallery of **lesson-flow template cards**
  (§3 / §4). Each card shows the template name, a one-line description, and its ordered
  section chips (e.g. *I Do · We Do · You Do · Debrief*). Clicking a card expands a
  full preview of every section and its guiding prompt.
- **Recommended** badge on *Gradual Release (I Do / We Do / You Do)* for a general
  Grade 5 classroom; *4-Part Math Workshop* offered as the suggested math alternative.
- A **Build my own** card opens the template editor (§3.3).
- The chosen template becomes the **account-wide default** for new lessons in academic
  subjects. It can be overridden per subject and per lesson later.
- *Backed by:* `LessonSectionTemplate` + a default pointer (see §5).

### Step 6 — Standards  *(skippable)*
- "Which standards do you align to?" — framework list: **CCSS** (preselected for Grade
  5, US), US state standards, IB PYP/MYP, Cambridge, **MOEHE (Qatar)**, ADEK, British
  National Curriculum, Australian, NZ, or **Custom (CSV)**.
- Multi-select. **Skip for now** is prominent — standards can be added in Settings.
- *Backed by:* `StandardsFramework` / `GradeFrameworkAssignment`.

### Step 7 — Your daily schedule  *(skippable, light version)*
- "Lay out a typical day" — an ordered list; the teacher adds time blocks and assigns
  each to a subject (or marks it a non-academic block). Times optional.
- Kept deliberately light here; the full time-block editor lives in Settings. If A/B or
  a numbered cycle was chosen in Step 3, each block gets an A/B / cycle-day selector.
- **Skip for now** offered — the Weekly view works without it; only the Schedule view
  needs it.
- *Backed by:* `Time Block` (already in the spec).

### Step 8 — You're set
- Summary card: *"Grade 5 · Sun–Thu week · 8 subjects · CCSS · Gradual Release default."*
- **Enter the planner** button.
- Soft secondary CTA: *"Import last year's plan?"* (links to Curriculum Import — not
  blocking).
- *Backed by:* `OnboardingState` marked complete.

**Order rationale:** grade → week → rotation must come before subjects and schedule
(they define the calendar). Subjects must come before the schedule (blocks reference
subjects) and before the template step (template applies to academic subjects).
Standards and schedule are last because they are skippable.

## 3. The lesson-flow template system  (new concept)

The planning doc's lesson model has three flat rich-text fields (`directions`,
`learning_objectives`, `notes`) and **no concept of lesson sections or templates** —
the old spec explicitly deferred "section-level" structure to Phase 4. This plan
introduces it now, because the user wants every lesson built from a structured,
teacher-chosen flow.

### 3.1 Concept

A **lesson-flow template** is an ordered list of named **sections** (e.g. *I Do*, *We
Do*, *You Do*). When a teacher creates a lesson in an **academic** subject, the lesson
is pre-populated with that subject's template sections. Each section in a real lesson is
an editable unit:

- a **section label** (from the template, renamable per lesson),
- a **rich-text body** (the teacher's content; the empty state shows the template's
  guiding prompt as placeholder text),
- an **attached-resources** list (links/files — same resource model as the rest of the
  app), so resources live *with the section they belong to*, not in one undifferentiated
  pile.

### 3.2 Editing sections — per lesson vs. per template

Two independent editing scopes (the user's explicit requirement):

- **Per lesson:** in any single lesson the teacher can rename, reorder, add, remove, or
  empty a section, and attach resources to it. This changes only that lesson.
- **Per template:** editing the template (rename/reorder/add/remove sections, change a
  section's guiding prompt) changes what **future** lessons get. Existing lessons are
  not retroactively rewritten (their sections are already independent instances). An
  optional "apply to existing empty sections" action can be offered later.

Lesson-level Master/Personal forking still operates at the **lesson** level — when a
lesson forks, its sections fork with it as content. Independent section-level forking is
a possible later enhancement, not Phase 1.

### 3.3 The template editor & custom flows

- **Built-in library:** 15 ready-made templates (§4), each with named sections + guiding
  prompts. Read-only originals; "Use" copies one in, "Duplicate & edit" forks it.
- **Custom flows:** a teacher can build a template from scratch — add sections, name
  them, write a guiding prompt for each, mark some required, set the order. A saved
  custom template appears in the gallery and can be set as the default so it is applied
  to **every new lesson event** automatically.
- **Scope:** templates are `personal` by default; a lead can publish a `team` template.
  Built-ins are `builtin` scope.
- Templates are pickable in onboarding (Step 5), in Settings, per subject, and per
  lesson ("change lesson structure").

### 3.4 Non-academic subjects

Subjects marked **Non-academic** in Step 4 do not use templates at all. Their
lessons/blocks are a simple title + optional note + optional resources — no sections,
no objectives, no standards. This keeps lunch, recess, assemblies, and morning meeting
from carrying machinery they don't need.

## 5. Data-model additions

New entities / fields (high-level — schema lands with the Supabase backend):

**Onboarding & school config**
- `SchoolWeekConfig` — `weekdays` (ordered array of weekday ids), `preset`
  (sun_thu / mon_fri / custom). Drives every calendar surface.
- `SchoolYear` gains `cycle_type` (none / ab / numbered), `cycle_length`,
  `cycle_anchor_date` (generalizes the spec's `active_cycle_pattern`).
- `OnboardingState` — per teacher: `completed_steps`, `completed_at`. Also a
  school-level `setup_complete` flag.

**Subject**
- `Subject` gains `color` (palette swatch id), `is_academic` (boolean),
  `default_section_template_id` (FK, nullable — falls back to the account default),
  `display_order`.

**Lesson-flow templates**
- `LessonSectionTemplate` — `id`, `name`, `description`, `scope`
  (builtin / team / personal), `owner_id`, `grade_level_id` (nullable).
- `LessonSectionTemplateItem` — `template_id`, `label`, `guiding_prompt`,
  `is_required`, `display_order`.
- `LessonSection` — per lesson event: `id`, `lesson_event_id`, `lesson_event_type`
  (master / personal), `source_template_item_id` (nullable), `label`, `body`
  (rich text), `display_order`. Resources attach to a section via the existing
  resource model keyed by `lesson_section_id`.
- A default-template pointer at the account level (on `TeacherUIState` or a school
  setting).

**Note:** for academic lessons, `LessonSection[]` supersedes the flat `directions`
field. `learning_objectives`, `notes`, `standards`, and `resources` remain on the
lesson. Non-academic lesson/blocks keep only title + note.

**Classes / sections (flagged gap, not in this build):** the spec has only a
`class_id` placeholder on `EventDayOrderOverride` and no `Class` entity. Onboarding
asks for *subjects*, not student sections. If multi-section tracking is wanted, a
minimal `Class` entity is a separate decision — see §8.

## 6. What we borrow from competitors & screenshots

- **commonplanner.com** — the linear setup with clear section headers; drag-to-reorder
  the subject/class list; per-row day toggles; "this isn't a planning block" toggle
  (our Academic/Non-academic toggle is the sharper version of this).
- **Planbook** — collect school-year dates + schedule type *before* subjects; full
  support for numbered cycle schedules (Day 1–20).
- **Planboard/Chalk** — color-code subjects during setup; attach standards to setup,
  not just per-lesson.
- **Toddle** — its 3×3 multimodal attach grid → our "add resource" picker; but avoid
  Toddle's weeks-long admin onboarding — ours is self-serve, < 10 min.
- **Curriculum.cc screenshots** — teacher-persona cards as an optional friendly
  framing; year-timeline with colored unit bars.
- **"Lesson Event Resources" screenshots** — confirm teachers already think in named
  lesson sections (*Starter · Main Activity · Extension · Resources · Reflection*) and
  attach resources *per section* — direct validation of §3.
- **commonplanner's per-class template gallery** — we make it an account-level default
  (one choice, not one-per-class), overridable later.

## 7. Build phasing

A suggested sequence (each is an increment; agent-team friendly):

1. **Onboarding shell & routing** — `/onboarding` route, wizard chrome (progress bar,
   Back/Continue, resumable state), `OnboardingState`. Steps 0–3 (welcome, grade,
   school week, rotation).
2. **Subject setup (Step 4)** — the subject list with name + color picker +
   academic/non-academic toggle + add/reorder/remove. `Subject.color` / `is_academic`.
3. **Lesson-flow template system** — `LessonSectionTemplate` model, the 15 built-in
   templates, the template gallery + preview, the template editor (custom flows).
4. **Default-template step (Step 5)** + wiring the chosen template into lesson creation.
5. **Lesson sections in the lesson UI** — render `LessonSection[]` (label + rich text +
   per-section resources) in the lesson detail / editor; per-lesson reorder/add/remove.
6. **Standards & schedule steps (6–7)** + summary (Step 8).
7. **Make calendar surfaces config-driven** — Weekly grid columns, Daily day list,
   Schedule derive days from `SchoolWeekConfig`; replace the hard-coded `WEEK_DAYS`
   mock.

Phasing note vs. the roadmap: onboarding + a basic lesson-section model are reasonable
**Phase 1A** (the app is unusable without setup). The full template editor and custom
flows can be **Phase 1B** if 1A timeline pinches — 1A could ship with the built-in
library only (no custom editor).

## 8. Open questions / decisions needed

1. **School vs. teacher setup split.** For the single-school beta, is there one
   combined wizard, or a lead-only school-setup pass (grade levels, standards, team
   subjects) followed by a light teacher pass? Recommendation: **one wizard** for the
   beta; revisit when multi-school.
2. **Classes/sections.** *Resolved — support both.* The app supports planning by
   subject alone **and** optional named class sections (5A/5B/5C). The onboarding class
   step is optional; a minimal `Class` entity is added but stays dormant unless a
   teacher opts in to section-level planning.
3. **Subject color authority.** Onboarding sets subject colors; the design system calls
   the subject→color mapping "locked team-wide." Confirm: the **first teacher / lead**
   sets the canonical colors at onboarding, others inherit, Appearance settings can
   re-tune per teacher. (Assumed in this plan.)
4. **Template default scope.** *Resolved — one account-wide default*, with per-subject
   and per-lesson override available later.
5. **Retroactive template edits.** When a template changes, should existing lessons'
   untouched/empty sections update? Recommendation: **no** by default; offer an opt-in
   "apply to empty sections" action later.
6. **Section-level forking.** Phase 1 forks at the lesson level (sections ride along).
   Independent per-section Master/Personal forking — future enhancement, confirm it is
   not needed for the beta.
